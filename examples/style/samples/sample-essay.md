# Sample style essay

I used to think good writing was about precision. I now think it's about
restraint. The sentence that earns its place is the one you were tempted
to cut and didn't — but only because cutting it would have broken something
specific, not because you were attached to the phrasing.

The hard part of academic writing is not the claims. It is the connective
tissue: the sentences that stitch a claim to the evidence, and the evidence
to the limits. Students tend to over-invest in the claim and under-invest
in the stitch. The result reads like a pile of strong sentences that
refuse to form a paragraph.

When I revise, I go through three passes. First, cut adjectives. Second,
make every sentence end on the word that carries the most weight. Third,
ask of each paragraph: what would a hostile reader object to here, and
have I pre-empted it? If the answer to the third question is no, the
paragraph isn't finished.
